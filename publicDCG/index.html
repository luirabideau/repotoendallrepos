About the Collection

The Hawaiian and Pacific Collections house a vast array of unique and specialized materials, encompassing audiovisual (AV) materials, maps, and microfilm records. These collections provide invaluable resources for research, education, and preservation, with a focus on the cultural and historical significance of Hawaiʻi and the broader regions of Polynesia, Melanesia, and Micronesia.

Our AV collection includes rare recordings, documentaries, and archival footage that capture the voices, stories, and traditions of the Pacific Islands. The map collection offers detailed cartographic records that highlight geographic, cultural, and linguistic shifts over time. Meanwhile, the microfilm archive preserves historical documents, periodicals, and government records critical to understanding the history and development of the region. Explore these materials through our search tools, tailored to streamline your research and discovery process. For additional information on access and use, visit the About page.
